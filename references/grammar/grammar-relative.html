Defining Relative Clauses

Use: Essential information, no commas

Pronouns: who (people), which (things), that (both), where (places)

Example: "The book that I bought is interesting." / "The man who called is my friend."

Non-Defining Relative Clauses

Use: Extra information, use commas

Pronouns: who, which, where (NOT that)

Example: "My brother, who lives in Paris, is a doctor."

Relative Pronouns

Who: People (subject) / Whom: People (object)

Which: Things / That: People or things (defining only)

Where: Places / Whose: Possession

Omitting Relative Pronouns

Rule: Can omit when pronoun is object of the clause

Example: "The book (that) I bought" / "The person (who) I met"

Cannot omit: When pronoun is subject ("The man who called")
