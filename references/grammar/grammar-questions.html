Questions

â† Back to Grammar Rules

Yes/No Questions

Form: Auxiliary verb + subject + main verb

Example: "Do you like coffee?" / "Are you coming?" / "Have you finished?"

Use: Questions expecting yes or no answers

WH-Questions

Form: WH-word (what, where, when, who, why, how) + auxiliary + subject

Example: "What do you do?" / "Where are you going?" / "Why did you leave?"

Use: Questions asking for specific information

Tag Questions

Form: Statement + comma + opposite auxiliary + pronoun

Example: "You like coffee, don't you?" / "She's coming, isn't she?"

Use: Confirming information, seeking agreement

Question Words

What: Things / Where: Places / When: Time

Who: People / Why: Reasons / How: Manner/Quantity

Example: "What time is it?" / "How much does it cost?"
