Passive Voice

â† Back to Grammar Rules

Basic Passive

Form: Subject + be (am/is/are) + past participle

Example: "The car is washed." / "English is spoken here."

Use: When the action is more important than who does it

Past Passive

Form: Subject + was/were + past participle

Example: "The letter was sent yesterday." / "The windows were broken."

Use: Past actions where the doer is unknown or unimportant

Passive with Agent

Form: Subject + be + past participle + by + agent

Example: "The book was written by Shakespeare." / "The cake was made by my mother."

Use: When you want to mention who performed the action

Modal Passive

Form: Subject + modal + be + past participle

Example: "It must be done." / "The work can be finished today."

Use: Expressing possibility, necessity, or obligation in passive
